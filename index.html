Handicap Apparatus for Normal Disarticulation (H.A.N.D.)

The H.A.N.D. is an artificial hand prosthesis that relies on use of preexisting tendons and muscles to control the fingers of the prosthesis. It will most benefit amputees with a fully intact forearm up to the wrist, from which the extensor digitorum and flexor pollicis longus tendons can still be used.

Though the target market for this prosthesis seems scarce, a study in 2005 found that more than 10,000 Americans had undergone wrist disarticulation amputaion, the surgery that this prosthesis benefits the most.
