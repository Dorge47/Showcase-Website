Target Market

This product will benefit amputees that have undergone disarticulation amputation (the amputation of the hand at the wrist).

In order to use the device, the extensor digitorum tendons (control primary four fingers) and flexor pollicis longus tendon (controls thumb) must still be intact.

Though the target market for this prosthesis seems scarce, a study in 2005 found that more than 10,000 Americans had undergone wrist disarticulation amputaion. Given that only 24-56% of amputees even get a prosthesis to help them, it is evident that there is a need for this product.
